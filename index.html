Online Classes for Children

What kind of online programming are 3rd party platforms offering to children and parents?

This is a survey conducted on May 21st through May 24th of 2020 to evaluate the costs and benefits of various online learning platforms. The goal of this survey was to identify any gaps in the current online course offerings.

Summary

Methodology

A total of 37 children's museums were reviewed for this survey. Of these 37 museums, 29 were found to have some form of virtual programming. These museums were chosen from two sources:

  • 22 museums in California from the Wikipedia list of children's museums.
  • 15 museums in the United States from a Parents.com list of the best children's museums.

These sources were chosen because the goal of this survey is to provide virtual programming insight for the Children's Creativity Museum (CCM) in San Francisco, so it made sense to look at similar museums (i.e. in California and "the best"). For the museums that offered virtual programming, information about the content, educators involved, cost, and technology used were gathered from the museums' websites.


The Main Takeaways


Parents are excited about live classes in which their children can interact with other people.
There is already an abundance of content on each platform, but niche topics are very popular.
3rd party learning platforms take a substantial cut of class profits.
Outschool appears to be the most popular platform for live, online classes.
